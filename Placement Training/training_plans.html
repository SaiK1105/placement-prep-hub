🗺️ Training Plans

Written 22 July 2026, after going 0-for-5 on OAs with prediction-based cramming. The fix: volume + exam-tier practice. Two paths — the sustainable contract, and the one-week push. Check boxes as you go; progress saves in this browser.

⚖️ Non-negotiables (both plans)

  • Typed & submitted — every problem written in full in a real editor and submitted. Reading solutions ≠ reps.
  • Timer on everything. 25-min cap per DSA problem: cap hit → read the approach only → close it → type the full solution from blank.
  • Misses get root causes, logged in the mistake log — never shrugs.
  • Cold mocks are the only readiness signal we trust. No more "feels ready".
  • Company prep = format & logistics recon only (platform, sections, allowed languages) + one format-matched mock 2 days out. No content-prediction cramming, ever again.
  • Language policy: Python only through Hell Week + Contract weeks 1–4 (one language = maximum fluency). Weeks 5–8 add 15 min/day Java revival — re-type one already-solved problem in Java. If a company ever locks Java, that company's format-matched mock is done in Java.

Honest math: expertise ≈ 300 reps. Hell Week buys ~40, the Contract buys ~170 more. That's the whole strategy — there is no shortcut, only the ladder.

🔥 Hell Week ~6–7 h/day · 7 days

Daily shape: Morning 5 DSA (25-min caps) · Midday 7 SQL (executed, not eyeballed) · Afternoon re-solve yesterday's misses from a BLANK editor + 30 min timed aptitude · Evening odd days = cold mini-mock, even days = 2 hard problems with tutor. Sleep 7+ hrs — it's part of the plan. Miss a day → drop into Plan A. No making up.

⚠️ If Honeywell Round 2 happens (in-person hackathon, 25–26 Jul), those days ARE the hackathon — Hell Week pauses and resumes after. That's a win, not a miss.

📜 The Contract ~2–2.5 h/day · 8 weeks

The daily loop, every single day: 3 DSA problems from the 210-tracker in ladder order (~90 min) + 20 min timed aptitude. Mon/Wed/Fri: +30 min SQL (LeetCode SQL 50, in order — continue past Hell Week's progress). Sunday: cold 2-hour mock (MCQ block + 2–3 DSA + 1 SQL), honestly scored; misses become next week's drills. A short day is 1 problem — never 0.

🎯 Milestones — how we know it's working

  • End of Week 2: arrays/strings/hashing clean at medium tier
  • End of Week 4: 2-of-3 mediums cleared in Sunday mocks, consistently
  • End of Week 6: medium-hards entering the mix without panic
  • End of Week 8: full OA sims at real difficulty, above cut-line pace — ready for the September wave
