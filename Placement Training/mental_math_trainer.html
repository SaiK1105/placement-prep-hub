The only 5 moves you need

Every placement calculation is one of these five. Learn the move, not the answer.

1
The 10% anchor — to find any % of a number, first find 10% (just shift the decimal), then scale.
30% of 240 → 10% is 24 → ×3 → 72
5% of 380 → 10% is 38 → half → 19
35% of 400 → 10%=40 → ×3=120, +half of 40=20 → 140
1% works the same: shift twice. 7% of 300 → 1%=3 → ×7 → 21.
2
Percent = multiplier — you already know this one (you got 1.5 × 0.9 = 135 right!).
+20% → ×1.2 · −25% → ×0.75 · chain them for successive changes.
And for two changes there's the instant formula: a + b + ab/100 → +40, −20 → 40−20−8 = +12%.
3
Break it apart — big multiplications become small ones.
23 × 14 → 23×10 + 23×4 → 230 + 92 → 322
18 × 12 → 18×10 + 18×2 → 180+36 → 216
Always left to right: big chunk first, then the small one. Left-to-right is HOW mental math works — school taught you right-to-left because paper is patient. Your head isn't.
4
Round, then fix — compute with a friendly number, then repair the damage.
49 × 6 → 50×6 = 300, minus one 6 → 294
397 + 258 → 400+258 = 658, minus 3 → 655
75 × 12 → 75×12 is ¾ of 1200 → 900
5
Halve & double — trade a hard factor for an easy one.
16 × 35 → 8 × 70 → 560
25 × 48 → 100 × 12 → 1200 (25 = 100/4, so quarter the other number)
5 × anything → ×10, then halve. 5 × 86 → 860/2 → 430

⚡ Speed tricks by operation

Multiply by 11

52 × 11 → write 5_2, middle = 5+2 → 572. Carry if needed: 68×11 → 6_(14)_8 → 748.

Multiply by 9 / 99

47 × 9 → 47×10 − 47 → 423. Same idea: ×99 = ×100 − once.

Squares ending in 5 (magic one)

35² → first digit × (itself+1), stick 25 on: 3×4=12 → 1225. 75² → 7×8=56 → 5625. Ten seconds saved, every time.

Squares near a known one

21² = 20² + 20 + 21 = 441. 19² = 20² − 20 − 19 = 361. (n±1)² without the formula stress.

Percent flip

x% of y = y% of x. 4% of 75 feels hard → 75% of 4 = 3. Instant.

Division by 5, 25, 125

÷5 → ×2 then ÷10: 435 ÷ 5 → 870/10 → 87. ÷25 → ×4 ÷100. ÷125 → ×8 ÷1000.

The exam meta-trick: estimate → eliminate

MCQs don't need exact answers. 289 × 31? That's ≈ 290×30 ≈ 8700 — if only one option is near 8700, you're done. Options are your friends; check them before calculating precisely.

📅 The 5-minute daily routine

Mental math is a muscle, not knowledge — 5 minutes daily beats 2 hours once. Until placements end:

  • Min 1–2: Drill Arena, "% of number" — beat yesterday's streak.
  • Min 3: One column of the fraction↔% table out loud, from memory.
  • Min 4: Five two-digit multiplications (Arena "2-digit ×").
  • Min 5: One "profit chain" (Arena) — the exact skill Presidio MCQs test.
Real-life reps (free practice): mess prices (add your tray total before the cashier does), cricket strike rates, splitting bills, discount tags in shops. Every number you see is a rep.
Exam-day rule: if a calculation takes more than 3 steps in your head, you're solving it the wrong way — look for the trick (fraction form, round-and-fix, or estimate-and-eliminate). Placement questions are DESIGNED to have a shortcut.
