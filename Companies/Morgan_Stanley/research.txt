Morgan Stanley Apprenticeship OA — What To Expect
OA Format & Sections
Platform: SHL is used for the Online Assessment. "It’s on SHL."

Sections: Expect three main parts: coding (DSA), debugging/fix-the-code, and aptitude/office-roles. "Three sections: debugging (7 questions, easy), aptitude (logical reasoning, quant, verbal, significantly harder), and coding (3 medium to hard problems)."

Debugging / Fix The Code: Debugging/fix-the-code appears and is typically easy if you practice common AMCAT-style bugs. "The 'Fix the Code' Part: You’ll have to fix bugs in existing code... these are usually the same ones that pop up in AMCAT tests."

Coding (DSA) Expectations & Weight
Number & Difficulty: Usually three coding problems; difficulty ranges from medium to hard and weight on shortlisting is high. "You’ll probably get 3 questions. When I took it, it was 2 DP (Dynamic Programming) problems and 1 Tree problem."

Performance Criteria: Partial solutions with good logic can still advance you — fully solving all problems isn’t strictly necessary. "I did not finish all the coding problems but still advanced. Partial solutions with sound logic appear to be evaluated rather than just pass/fail."

Common Topics: DP, trees, arrays/strings, two-pointer/greedy show up in posts about the process. "…2 DP (Dynamic Programming) problems and 1 Tree problem." and "medium dsa question(dp, 2-pointer,greedy,etc..)"

Aptitude & Other Non-DSA Parts
Aptitude Difficulty: Aptitude (math/logic) can be medium-to-hard but usually receives less focus than DSA. "The math/logic part is medium difficulty—don't waste too much time studying for it."

Office-Roles / Misc: There’s an "office roles" or personality section; it’s low priority — answer the ones you do correctly. "There’s also a boring 'office roles' section. Don't worry if you don't finish it; just make sure the ones you do answer are correct."

Shortlisting, Fairness & Outcomes
Shortlisting Factors: OA performance matters, but college CGPA / shortlist processes also influence interview invitations. "Morgan stanley shortlists people with high cg, so if you are in top 10-20 in batch with good cg then you have good chance"

Integrity Concerns: Cheating concerns were raised by students, but opinions vary; some say cheating occurs, others say most students are fair and cheating risks permanent block. "Simple answer. NO. It's not fair at all. And don't expect it to get better. Cheating is common everywhere now..." and "If you get caught once, then you’re permanently blocked, so don’t worry 😅"

Stipend & PPO: Community reports mention a ~1 L/month stipend for apprenticeship and performance-based PPOs are common. "1L/Month stipend" and "Performance based ppo."

Interview Follow-ups After OA
Interview Rounds: If shortlisted, expect 1–2 technical rounds plus HR/values round; interviews test resume, core CS subjects, SQL, and medium DSA. "Round 1 (Technical): ... basic DSA, OOPs, and SQL." and "interview: complete knowledge of resume, strong understanding on core subjects(oops, os, dbms), some sql query(leetcode medium), medium dsa question..."

Notepad / Live Coding: You may be asked to code on a notepad/whiteboard and should be ready to ask for clarification/hints. "The Notepad Test: They’ll make you write code on a blank notepad. If you get stuck, just ask for help!"

Evaluation Nuances: Companies may take weeks/months to issue offers; background checks and portal statuses can remain "reviewing" for long periods. "It took a little over 3 months for them to send the offer letter and then there's a background check process." and "my application status on the portal still shows 'reviewing your candidacy'..."

Quick Preparation Checklist
Practice Hard DP & Tree problems: Focus Striver/hard DP + tree problems. "How to prep: Just grind the Hard DP and Tree problems from Striver’s sheet."

Debugging/AMCAT-style bugs: Solve common code-fix/debug questions. "You’ll have to fix bugs in existing code... these are usually the same ones that pop up in AMCAT tests."

Aptitude Basics: Polish medium-level quant/logic problems but prioritize DSA. "The math/logic part is medium difficulty—don't waste too much time studying for it."

Resume & CS Fundamentals: Be ready to discuss resume lines and core topics (OOPs, OS, DBMS, SQL). "if you mention anything in your resume you need to have strong knowledge of it."
