Block 1 · Percentages + Profit & Loss

10:00
📖 Your PDF: Shortcuts #18–23 (Percentage) and #24–29 (Profit, Loss & Discount)

2-min trick recap

  • P% = (SP−CP)/CP × 100 — loss% divides by CP too (your PDF's #24 uses SP−CP over CP; watch questions that sneak in "of SP").
  • Two successive changes (#25): net% = a + b + ab/100 (profit +, loss −). Mark up 40%, discount 20% → 40 − 20 − 8 = +12%. Instant answer, no CP needed.
  • Chain of sales (#26): final SP = CP × (multipliers). 10% loss → ×0.9, 40% profit → ×1.4.
  • x% of y = y% of x — 8% of 50 = 50% of 8 = 4. Free speed.
  • Fraction ↔ % anchors: 1/8=12.5%, 1/6≈16.67%, 1/3≈33.3%, 3/8=37.5%, 5/8=62.5%.

Drill (8 min · ~80 sec each)

  1. An item marked 60% above cost is sold at 25% discount. Profit %?
  2. A trader sells two watches at ₹960 each — one at 20% profit, one at 20% loss. Net result?
  3. Price of sugar rises 25%. By what % must a family cut consumption to keep spending equal?
  4. A sells to B at 20% profit, B sells to C at 10% loss. C paid ₹5400. A's cost price?
  5. After two successive discounts of 10% and 20%, a shirt costs ₹1440. Marked price?
  6. 65% of a number is 156. What is 35% of it?
🔑 Block 1 answers

1) ×1.6 ×0.75 = 1.2 → 20% profit (or #25: 60−25−15=20)
2) Same SP, equal ±20% → always a loss: #25 gives −(20²/100) = 4% loss
3) Increase 25% = ×5/4 → cut = 1−4/5 = 20%
4) CP × 1.2 × 0.9 = 5400 → CP = 5400/1.08 = ₹5000
5) MP × 0.9 × 0.8 = 1440 → MP = ₹2000
6) number = 156/0.65 = 240 → 35% = 84 (or trick: 35% = 156×(35/65) = 84)

Block 2 · Ratio & Proportion + Averages

10:00
📖 Your PDF: Shortcuts #1–8 (Ratio & Proportion), #13–17 (Average), #30–33 (Ages)

2-min trick recap

  • Splitting in ratio (#1): a:b share of N = a/(a+b) × N. Three-way works the same.
  • Ratio + LCM/HCF: numbers = 3x, 5x → LCM = 15x, HCF = x. (Presidio mock had exactly this.)
  • Average shift (#13–17): one member leaves and average drops → removed value = old avg + n(change) pattern. Total-first thinking beats formula memorizing: work with sums.
  • Ages (#30–33): translate English → equation immediately. "5 years ago A was twice B" → A−5 = 2(B−5).
  • Weighted mix: if averages of groups differ, answer sits closer to the bigger group (alligation, #10–12).

Drill (8 min)

  1. Divide ₹1440 among A, B, C in ratio 2:3:4. C gets?
  2. Two numbers are in ratio 4:7, HCF is 6. Their LCM?
  3. Average of 11 results is 50. Average of first six is 49, last six is 52. The 6th result?
  4. The average age of a class of 30 is 15. Including the teacher it becomes 15.5. Teacher's age?
  5. A is twice as old as B. 10 years ago A was four times B. A's present age?
  6. Ratio of boys to girls is 7:5. If there are 84 boys, total students?
🔑 Block 2 answers

1) 4/9 × 1440 = ₹640
2) Numbers 24 & 42 → LCM = 168 (= 4×7×6)
3) 6×49 + 6×52 − 11×50 = 294+312−550 = 56
4) 31×15.5 − 30×15 = 480.5−450 = 30.5 years
5) A=2B; A−10 = 4(B−10) → 2B−10 = 4B−40 → B=15, A=30
6) 84/7 = 12 → total 12×12 = 144

Block 3 · Time & Work

10:00
📖 Your PDF: Shortcuts #50–60 (Time & Work), #61+ (Pipes & Cisterns — same logic, filling = work)

2-min trick recap

  • Rate thinking: A in 12 days → A does 1/12 per day. Together: add rates. (Presidio mock Q2 was exactly this.)
  • LCM method (faster than fractions): A=12d, B=18d → take work = LCM 36 units. A does 3/day, B does 2/day → together 5/day → 36/5 = 7.2 days. No fraction juggling.
  • Work ∝ time, ∝ men (#50–52): the universal formula M₁D₁/W₁ = M₂D₂/W₂ solves "how many men/days" instantly.
  • Leaving mid-way: compute work done → remaining work → remaining rate.
  • Pipes: inlet = +rate, leak/outlet = −rate. Same LCM method.

Drill (8 min)

  1. A does a work in 15 days, B in 20 days. Together?
  2. 12 men build a wall in 18 days. How many days for 9 men?
  3. A and B together finish in 8 days; A alone in 12. B alone?
  4. A does a job in 10 days. After A works 4 days, B finishes the rest in 9 days. B alone would take?
  5. Pipe A fills a tank in 6 hrs, pipe B empties it in 9 hrs. Both open — time to fill?
  6. If 20 men working 8 hrs/day finish in 15 days, how many days for 24 men at 10 hrs/day?
🔑 Block 3 answers

1) LCM 60: 4+3=7/day → 60/7 ≈ 8.57 days
2) M₁D₁=M₂D₂: 12×18/9 = 24 days
3) 1/8 − 1/12 = 1/24 → 24 days
4) A did 4/10 → 6/10 left in 9 days → B rate = (3/5)/9 = 1/15 → 15 days
5) 1/6 − 1/9 = 1/18 → 18 hours
6) 20×8×15 = 24×10×D → D = 2400/240 = 10 days

Block 4 · Speed, Distance & Trains

10:00
📖 Your PDF: Shortcuts #34–39 (Time, Speed & Distance), #40–45 (Trains), #46–49 (Boats)

2-min trick recap

  • km/h ↔ m/s: × 5/18 one way, × 18/5 back. 72 km/h = 20 m/s. Memorize: 36→10, 54→15, 72→20, 90→25.
  • Train past a pole (#40): time = L_train / speed. Past a platform (#41): (L_train + L_platform) / speed.
  • Relative speed (#34–35, #42–43): opposite directions → add; same direction → subtract. Crossing trains: total length / relative speed.
  • Average speed for equal distances (#36): 2xy/(x+y) — never (x+y)/2!
  • Boats (#46–49): downstream = b+s, upstream = b−s; boat = (down+up)/2, stream = (down−up)/2.

Drill (8 min)

  1. A 180 m train at 54 km/h crosses a pole in?
  2. A 200 m train crosses a 300 m platform in 25 s. Speed in km/h?
  3. Two trains, 150 m and 250 m, run at 45 and 27 km/h in opposite directions. Time to cross?
  4. A car covers half a trip at 40 km/h, half at 60 km/h. Average speed?
  5. A boat goes 30 km downstream in 2 hrs and back in 3 hrs. Speed of the stream?
  6. A thief 100 m ahead runs at 8 km/h; police chase at 10 km/h. Catch time?
🔑 Block 4 answers

1) 54→15 m/s → 180/15 = 12 s
2) 500 m / 25 s = 20 m/s = 72 km/h
3) Rel = 72 km/h = 20 m/s → 400/20 = 20 s
4) 2×40×60/100 = 48 km/h
5) Down 15, up 10 → stream = (15−10)/2 = 2.5 km/h
6) Rel = 2 km/h = 5/9 m/s → 100/(5/9) = 180 s = 3 min

Block 5 · Number Series & Coding-Decoding

10:00
📖 Your PDF: Progressions section · plus "Series (aptitude) q_a" PDF in the same folder

2-min trick recap

  • Series checklist (run in order, 10 sec each): ① differences constant? ② differences of differences? ③ ratio (×2, ×3, ×1.5)? ④ squares/cubes (±1)? ⑤ alternating/two interleaved series? ⑥ primes?
  • Know by sight: squares to 20² (400), cubes to 12³ (1728), primes to 100.
  • Coding-decoding: write A=1…Z=26 on rough paper FIRST — worth 30 seconds. Check: shift (+k), reverse (A↔Z: sum=27), position values.
  • Letter series: convert to numbers, then it's a number series.

Drill (8 min)

  1. 3, 7, 15, 31, 63, __ ?
  2. 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, __ ?
  3. 1, 4, 9, 61, 52, 63, __ ? (tricky — think digits)
  4. 5, 11, 23, 47, 95, __ ?
  5. If TABLE is coded as UBCMF, CHAIR is coded as?
  6. If REASON is coded as 5 and BELIEVED as 7, what is GOVERNMENT?
  7. J, F, M, A, M, __ ? (think outside numbers)
🔑 Block 5 answers

1) ×2+1 → 127
2) primes → 17
3) squares with digits reversed: 1,4,9,16→61,25→52,36→63,49→ 94
4) ×2+1 → 191
5) +1 each letter → DIBJS
6) letters − 1 → GOVERNMENT (10 letters) → 9
7) months: Jan Feb Mar Apr May → J (June)

Block 6 · Seating, Blood Relations & Syllogisms

10:00
📖 Your PDF folder: "Comprehensive interview material" & "500 most asked apti ques" cover these with worked examples

2-min trick recap

  • Seating: ALWAYS draw. Row → line of boxes; circle → clock face. Facing north: left = your left. Facing south: flipped! Fill certain positions first, then constraints.
  • Blood relations: build a tree, don't chain in your head. "My grandfather's only son" = my father. Then "daughter of my father" = my sister (Presidio mock Q8).
  • Syllogisms: draw Venn circles. "All A are B" = A inside B. "Some B are C" = overlap. A conclusion is valid only if it's true in EVERY possible diagram.
  • Direction puzzles: draw compass, track (x,y) from origin. Final distance = Pythagoras.

Drill (8 min)

  1. Six people in a row facing north: P is 3rd from left; Q is 2nd right of P; R is at an end but not next to Q; S is between P and Q. Where is R?
  2. Around a circular table, 5 friends face the center. A sits between B and C; D is to the immediate right of B. Who is to the immediate left of A? (take "right/left" as the seated person's own right/left)
  3. Pointing to a man, Sita says: "His mother is the only daughter of my mother." How is Sita related to the man?
  4. A walks 5 km north, turns right and walks 3 km, turns right again and walks 5 km. How far and in which direction from start?
  5. All cups are bottles. Some bottles are jugs. Conclusions: I. Some cups are jugs. II. Some jugs are bottles. Which follow(s)?
  6. A is B's brother, C is A's mother, D is C's father, E is B's son. How is D related to E?
🔑 Block 6 answers

1) Positions 1–6: P=3, Q=5, S=4, R at an end not next to Q → not 6 → position 1 (extreme left)
2) Circle facing center: D immediate right of B, A between B and C → going clockwise D, B, A, C, E → immediate left of A (= clockwise neighbor) is B… draw it! With 5 seats: right of B = D means D is B's right-hand neighbor; A between B and C ⇒ order around: D-B-A-C-E → A's left = B
3) "Only daughter of my mother" = Sita herself → she is his mother
4) Net: 3 km east of start → 3 km, East
5) I doesn't have to be true; II always true → only II
6) D = C's father = grandfather of A&B → E is B's son → D is E's great-grandfather
